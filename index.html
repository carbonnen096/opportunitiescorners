Top 10 Countries Offering Visa-Sponsored Jobs for Unskilled Workers in 2025

Explore global destinations actively hiring foreign workers with minimal experience—and how to apply for sponsored positions.

In 2025, many countries around the world are experiencing labor shortages in key industries such as agriculture, construction, hospitality, caregiving, and manufacturing. This shortage has opened doors for unskilled workers—individuals with minimal formal training or professional experience—to pursue visa-sponsored job opportunities abroad. These positions often come with benefits such as accommodation, relocation assistance, and full legal work sponsorship. For the latest updates and job leads in this space, platforms like opportunitiescorners are excellent resources to explore.

Canada

Why Canada? Canada faces labor shortages in rural and industrial sectors, especially in food production and caregiving.

Farmhands and fruit pickers Meat processing workers Caregivers and nursing aides Cleaning staff

Visa Program: Temporary Foreign Worker Program (TFWP)

How to Apply: Visit Job Bank Canada.

Australia

Why Australia? Agricultural and tourism sectors need seasonal and unskilled labor.

Fruit and vegetable pickers Kitchen assistants General laborers

Visa Program: Seasonal Worker Program PALM Scheme

How to Apply: Through licensed employers or DFAT.

Germany

Why Germany? Growing need for workers in manufacturing, logistics, and elder care.

Warehouse operators Factory workers Care assistants Maintenance staff

Visa Program: Opportunity Card Seasonal Employment Visa

How to Apply: Visit Make It in Germany.

United Kingdom

Why the UK? Agriculture and healthcare sectors urgently need workers post-Brexit.

Farm workers Hospitality staff Care home workers

Visa Program: Seasonal Worker Visa Health and Care Worker Visa

How to Apply: Check the UK's register of licensed sponsors.

Japan

Why Japan? Aged population and demand for support workers make Japan a top destination.

Caregivers Factory workers Construction assistants

Visa Program: Specified Skilled Worker (SSW) – Type 1

How to Apply: Pass a skills and Japanese language test.

Poland

Why Poland? Growing demand in construction and manufacturing industries.

Production line workers Warehouse assistants Facility workers

Visa Program: Type D National Visa EU Blue Card

How to Apply: Apply via the Ministry of Labour's portal.

New Zealand

Why New Zealand? Rural industries like farming and hospitality require seasonal workers.

Fruit harvesters Meat processing workers Hotel staff

Visa Program: Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV) Seasonal Work Schemes

How to Apply: Visit Work the Seasons.

United Arab Emirates (UAE)

Why UAE? Rapid growth in construction and hospitality drives labor demand.

Construction workers Hotel cleaners Security guards

Visa Program: Employment Visa (employer-sponsored)

How to Apply: Through verified recruitment agencies.

South Korea

Why South Korea? Employment Permit System allows unskilled foreign labor.

Manufacturing staff Fishermen Farmhands

Visa Program: E-9 Employment Permit Visa

How to Apply: Through EPS and Korean language test.

Portugal

Why Portugal? Streamlined immigration policies for seasonal and unskilled workers.

Farm laborers Food factory workers Warehouse staff

Visa Program: Seasonal Work Visa Residence Permit for Work

How to Apply: Via SEF or registered companies.

Tips for Success

  • Polish your resume and highlight reliability
  • Learn basic language skills
  • Apply early for seasonal roles
  • Use trusted platforms only
  • Watch out for scams—never pay for a job
